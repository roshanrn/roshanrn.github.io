I am a first-year PhD student at EPFL (IC), where I work with [Prof. George Candea](https://dslab.epfl.ch/people/candea/) in the [Dependable Systems Lab (DSLAB)](https://dslab.epfl.ch/).

My research lies at the intersection of storage systems, emerging storage and memory technologies, and performance modeling. I develop abstractions and simulation techniques for understanding how application behavior, storage-system design, and device-internal mechanisms interact at scale. My current work focuses on performance interfaces for SSD-backed systems: compact representations that expose the mechanisms governing performance and enable efficient reasoning and design-space exploration. 

If you are an EPFL student looking for a semester project, check out the [DSLAB projects page](https://dslab.epfl.ch/projects/) and follow the instructions on the page to apply. You are also welcome to contact me if you have a project idea related to my research interests. 

Before joining EPFL, I spent five years at Samsung Research, where I worked as a researcher and developer.  Prior to that, I completed my Master's in Mathematics and Bachelor's in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from BITS Pilani, Goa.
